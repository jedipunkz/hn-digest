LLM の関係。
ゾークの目標は、ダンジョンに入り、パズルを解いて宝物を見つけることです。その後、宝物を宝物ケースに預けることができます。宝物を見つけてケースに入れると、両方ともポイントがもらえます。アクセスしにくい場所に行ったり、パズルを解いたりすることでもポイントを獲得できます。これを行うには在庫を管理できる必要があります (重すぎるため、一度にすべてを運ぶことはできません)。騒がしい部屋を見つけたり、幽霊になった場合に人間に戻す方法を見つけたり、地下迷路など、このゲームをプレイしようとしている人や LLM にとって真の問題解決の課題となるその他のパズルを解決する必要があります。そして、LLM は実際にこれが苦手なようです。以下は、論文「AI で遊ぶ: 1977 年のテキストベースのアドベンチャー ゲーム Zork で最先端の大規模言語モデルはどのように実行されるか?」のプロットです。これは、現代の LLM がほとんどポイントを獲得しておらず、すべての平均が 350 ポイント中 50 ポイント程度にとどまっていることを示しています。また、まったく何もしないのにかなり多くの手を必要とします。
これは情けないことだ。上記論文より抜粋。これらすべてについてワイルドなのは、LLM がこのゲームで一発で勝てるはずだということです。ゾークは何十年も前から存在しています。それは、考えられるすべての反復でインターネット上に全文が掲載されてきました。ウォークスルー、スピード ラン ガイド、ヒントブック、そして文字通りゲームをプレイするあらゆる方法が、インターネット上で詳細に議論されています。それにもかかわらず、インターネット全体で訓練されたとされる LLM は、訓練データに含まれているはずのゲームをプレイするのに完全に失敗しています。それは非常に興味深い観察です。そして、Walter と私が Recurse Hub で話し合った運命の夜が、大規模言語モデル用の Zork ベースの推論評価ツールである zork-bench の誕生につながりました。
ゾール

k-bench は、LLM が Zork をプレイするためのハーネスを作成します。 Zork は、zulip-zork と同じバックエンドを使用してホストされます。 LLM には、メモを取ったり、地図を記録したりする機能などのツールが与えられます。 zork-bench の重要な要素は計画の調査です。プラチナ レンガを回収するなど、ゲームでポイントを獲得する賢い決断を下すには、在庫を整理し、騒々しい部屋の問題を解決し、騒がしい部屋から家の宝箱にプラチナ レンガを運ぶ必要があります。したがって、これらすべての決定にわたる LLM の選択と推論の連鎖により、これらの決定がどのように行われるかについての洞察が得られます。推論/思考テキストは、LLM 自体の評価にとって重要ではなくなり、代わりに、LLM がとるアクションを見て、それが合理的かどうかを判断できます。 LLM が 2 つの部屋の間を行ったり来たりするのに多くの時間を費やしている場合、それは推論が欠如していることを示しています。しかし、LLM が荷物を軽くするために宝箱に剣を入れ、騒がしい部屋に行き、パズルを解き、プラチナレンガを手に取り、宝箱に戻った場合、これは特定の問題を解決する (プラチナレンガを回収する) ための計画された一連のアクションを表すことになります。

LLM は、独自のマップを描画できるようになると、収束して同じことを行うようです。トークンのためにもっとお金が必要です。オリバー ツイストがやって来て、「お願いします。API クレジットをもっといただけますか?」と言ったと想像してみてください。 zork-bench による上記の結果は、ほとんどの LLM が 500 ターンにわたって、いくつかの宝物を収集し、部屋の半分未満を発見することを決して乗り越えることができないことをすでに示しています。その間、彼らはそのために膨大な量のトークンを費やします。以下では、前のターンが含まれるにつれてトークンが直線的に増加していることがわかりますが、驚くべきことは、「思考」と意思決定による大きなスパイクがあることです。これらについては今後の投稿で詳しく説明します。

zork a tale as old as time, or at least as old as computers.
John Aiken Apr 23, 2026 15 2 Share Growing up in the 90s I would go to the library and find books on computers. Most of these books were already out of date, containing printed Apple BASIC programs that you could try to copy in and get to work. My favorite one was an F-14 Tomcat simulator. I never got this to work. At the time my eight year old brain didn’t conceive that Applesoft BASIC was really just a different language from QBasic which I had on the 386 machine my father built and let me use. QBasic shipped with Gorillas, a Scorched Earth clone where the tanks are gorillas that throw bananas at each other. I have some plan to code by hand a Scorched Earth clone in Rust. If you haven’t heard back from me about this in a couple months feel free to send me a DM on Linkedin reminding me that you feel left out of the Scorched Earth Rust community that I have yet to build.
If you hit the sun with your banana it will have a frowny face. When reading all these old books and magazines from the public library you would hear about something called Zork. Zork was the original text adventure game created in the 1970s at MIT by Tim Anderson , Marc Blank , Bruce Daniels , and Dave Lebling for the PDP-10 mainframe computer . It is a game that is played almost entirely through text and imagination. It is a wonderful game where you traverse a labyrinthine underground in search of treasure. Many of the puzzles are not obvious, but this game has been part of computer culture since computers became a thing. There were many sequels, some with full motion video and graphics! , and the game itself created an entire genre of games called “text adventures” that influenced a lot of modern games and genres such as multi-user dungeons and rogue-like games .
I attended a Recurse Center batch recently. Recurse Center is a programming retreat where technically minded people congregate to build cool stuff. I think I better summed it up on LinkedIn where I said that it is basically Hackers, the movie, but in real life:
You too can find this post on linkedin by clicking this hyperlink . But at Recurse I met Mike Cugini who came in wanting to build a Rust implementation (see everyone likes Rust ) for the Z-machine emulator. The Z-machine is the original game engine that was built by Infocom , the video game company formed out of the creation of Zork the game, to build more text adventure games. This created a big interest in Zork across the Recurse batch and immediately we had a cohort of people like Fiona Chow and Kevan Hollbach who were interested in playing Zork regularly, understanding what Zork was as a game, and also building tools around Zork. You can see below Fiona debugging an Apple //e that is part of the Recurse Center’s historical computer library (they also have a NeXt computer omg).
Fiona Chow debugging an Apple //e while trying to download a copy of Zork from the internet over a streaming audio cable to write the game to floppy disk because we didn’t have a copy of Zork. Thank god Kevan Hollbach knew how to do this. The first thing this led to was the creation of zulip-zork , a zulip bot (zulip is a group chat program like slack or discord) that allows anyone within a zulip channel to play zork. Actually, I built this on the airplane traveling back from New York City to The Netherlands where I currently live. Missing my Zork friends at Recurse, I thought to myself, what is a Zork project I could do? Initially, I thought, well lets get LLMs to play Zork. But I decided that was too difficult at the moment, so then I decided what would be better than to continue playing Zork online? So, thank god the plane had free WiFi, I was able to hack together a zulip-zork bot built on top of Bot-Builder and docker-zork .

Essentially, dfrotz , a Z-machine emulator, runs in the docker container serving up Zork (it also supports other Infocom games), this in turn is connected to the bot which allows it to connect to a zulip channel, and you play by typing /game <commands> as you can see me doing above. This all runs on a VPS that I pay like 8$/month for that hosts random projects like this for me. This all worked quite well and so me and C Stravidis spent many many hours playing on Zulip together. There is a plan to replace the dfrotz emulator with Mike’s Rust implementation in the docker container.
But this question of LLMs playing Zork sat in the back of my mind still irritating me. Then one night, late at the Recurse Hub deep in conversation with Walter Min , Walter looked at me and just said, “zork-bench.” And I was inspired. That night I got home at maybe 3am and then woke up early, and got to work. By that evening I had a preliminary MVP for zork-bench to show at the weekly Recurse Center technical talks. In the talk I presented the LLM (anthropic opus 4.6, that was a quick way to spend 20$ in front of a bunch of people!) traversing the map of Zork and interacting with the game. And from that zork-bench has developed into a larger project attempting to assess the reasoning and problem solving abilities of LLMs.
The goal of Zork is to go into the dungeon and solve puzzles to find treasures. Treasures can then be deposited in the treasure case. And you get points both for finding treasures and putting them in the case. You also get points for going to hard to reach locations and solving puzzles. You need to be able to manage your inventory to do this (you can’t carry everything at once because it would be too heavy), there are puzzles to solve like figuring out the loud room or how to turn yourself back into a human if you become a ghost, an underground maze, and other things that present real problem solving challenges for a person or an LLM trying to play this game. And it seems like LLMs actually suck at this. Below you can see a plot from the paper, Playing With AI: How Do State-Of-The-Art Large Language Models Perform in the 1977 Text-Based Adventure Game Zork? , which shows that modern LLMs barely get any points at all, with an average across all of them sitting around 50 points out of 350. And they take quite a lot of moves to do nothing at all.
This is pathetic. Taken from the above mentioned paper. What is wild about all this is that LLMs should be able to one shot this game. Zork has been around for decades. It has been posted in full on the internet in every iteration imaginable. Walkthroughs, speed run guides, hint books, and literally every single way to play the game has been discussed at length on the internet. And yet, LLMs, allegedly trained on the whole of the internet, fail completely to play a game which should be in it’s training data. That is a very interesting observation. And so that fateful night of Walter and me talking together at the Recurse Hub led to zork-bench , a Zork-based reasoning evaluation tool for Large Language Models.
zork-bench creates a harness for LLMs to play Zork. Zork is hosted using the same backend as zulip-zork. LLMs are given tools such as the ability to take notes or record a map. An important component of zork-bench is investigating planning. In order to make clever decisions that get points in the game, such as recovering the platinum brick, one needs to organize their inventory, figure out the loud room problem, and transport the platinum brick from the loud room, to the treasure case in the house. Thus choices and reasoning chains for the LLM across all these decisions can give insight into how these decisions are made. The reasoning/thinking text becomes less important for evaluation of the LLM itself, instead we can look at the actions it takes and decide if they are reasonable. If an LLM spends a lot of time walking back and forth between two rooms, that shows a lack of reasoning. But if the LLM puts the sword in the treasure case to lighten its load, goes to the loud room, solves the puzzle, grabs the platinum brick, and returns to the treasure case, this would represent a planned sequence of actions to solve a specific problem (recover the platinum brick).
