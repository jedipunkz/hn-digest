Mason Alvarez had been farming almonds outside Fresno long enough to know drought doesn’t announce itself all at once. Water costs kept climbing, energy wasn’t getting cheaper, and every irrigation decision felt like a gamble. Most days he relied on experience, quick field checks, and instinct, but he could tell the margins were getting tighter each season.
When he partnered with Saturn Terminal, the first thing it gave him was clarity. The data collected made it obvious that two blocks he’d been watering the same way for years were behaving differently. One area was holding moisture longer than he expected, while another was drying out faster after hot, windy afternoons.
So Mason made small, targeted changes rather than overhauling everything. He trimmed runtime where the ground was already staying wet, and he adjusted timing where stress was building so he wasn’t wasting water or pumping longer than needed. Within a few weeks, the orchard looked more consistent, the pump ran less, and the savings showed up in his water and energy bills. This is proof that better information can be just as valuable as more inputs.
