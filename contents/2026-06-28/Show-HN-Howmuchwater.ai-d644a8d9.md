An interactive, animated look at how much water the average American uses at home — the 80–100 gallon-a-day range, fixture by fixture, plus the hidden water in everyday food. Built from USGS, EPA WaterSense and Water Research Foundation data.

Tired of the noise and FUD around AI water usage, created a little site for comparing AI usage with common daily activities.

Food : total green + blue + grey “virtual water” per typical serving, from the Water Footprint Network and Mekonnen & Hoekstra (2011, 2012) — global averages that vary widely by region and method.
AI : water evaporated to cool data centers plus the water used to generate their electricity, per query. Operators (OpenAI, Google) disclose on-site cooling only (~0.3 mL); independent estimates add power-plant water (~3–5 mL), and “thinking” models run far higher — from OpenAI (2025), Google (2025), Li et al. 2023 and Jegham et al. 2025. Figures swing widely with each data center's location and grid.
