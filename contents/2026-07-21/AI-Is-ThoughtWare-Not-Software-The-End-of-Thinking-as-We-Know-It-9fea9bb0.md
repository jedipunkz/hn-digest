AI is ThoughtWare, Not Software: The End of Thinking as We Know It
Private thought has always existed.
So far, thought has been private.
This has been one of the deepest constants of human civilization.
We have spoken, argued with, written books, and taught one another.
But thought itself has always belonged to a single person.
Every important decision in every invention, business, scientific discovery, work of art depended on one human being sitting alone with their own thoughts at least for a while.
I suspect we are living through the end of that era.
Of course, humans will not completely stop thinking.
But their thinking patterns will change in a historically significant way.
They may stop thinking about many mundane things altogether, such as picking something on their menu or the mini social interactions necessary to get your shopping done at the local supermarket.
Even about serious matters which used to take "sustained thinking" -- we may rarely "think alone" in the future.
This is not as strange as it sounds.
Modern people rarely travel long distances on foot.
We physically can walk long distances, of course, but usually we do it for health, or sport or community -- not out of need.
Civilization built a better mechanism for transportation, and walking across vast lands ceased to be the normal way of getting somewhere.
I suspect human thought is about to undergo the same transition.
Sustained thought, such as required for doing math and so on, is clearly tiring.
And majority of humans seem not to necessarily enjoy it.
Particularly without special training and great teachers and access to special perspectives which make the effort worth it for them, very few people do "hard thinking".
Even so-called mundane and fleeting thoughts, such as picking what to order from a restaurant menu is kind of an effortful operation.
And between the difficult research math problems to the problem of picking an item on the menu, we have many, many cognitive tasks we are subjected to presently, which if given an option to skip, many would gladly opt to skip.
So far, we have always done thoughts alone because there has never been another way.
John Tukey defined the term "Software" for the first time ever in 1958.
The first use of the word "Software" was made by John Tukey in 1958 in his paper "The Teaching of Concrete Mathematics", in "The American Mathematical Monthly"
Before software -- every task we wanted to get done required a unique machine of its own.
After software -- we were able to capture human thought as logical procedures in stored memory.
And get the hardware to execute it.
We needed a new word when software emerged because it described a new layer in the general architecture of technology.
Hardware described the machine; software described the instructions that animated it.
What is happening with AI now is -- technology is "moving up the stack".
AI occupies the "thought" layer.
AI can formulate large portions of human thought on its own, given human intent, direction and historical data.
Thought used to control software.
If AI can control software instead of human thought -- then what we have is something up the stack, something new.
This deserves a new category: ThoughtWare.
We now need another word. How about something like...
Yesterday the world looked like this:
Human Intent -> Human Thought -> Software -> Hardware
Your thought transformed that intention into software.
The software turned to hardware, and the final action got executed in the real world to produce results.
Tomorrow the architecture becomes something like:
Human Intent + Machine Thought -> Software -> Hardware
But the way thought or its equivalent gets formulated has changed. Also the "who" of thought is also a new pair of human and AI now.
