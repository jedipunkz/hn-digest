リュー議員とモラン議員、壊滅的な被害を引き起こす可能性のあるAIシステムにキルスイッチを義務付ける法案を提出
ワシントンD.C. — 本日、テッド・W・リュー下院議員(民主党、ロサンゼルス郡)とナサニエル・モラン下院議員(共和党、テキサス州)は、最も強力なAIシステムの開発者に対し、それらをスロットル、サスペンド、またはシャットダウンする技術的能力を維持することを義務付けるAIキルスイッチ法を導入した。
この法律はまた、国土安全保障省長官が商務長官および国家情報長官と協議の上、壊滅的な危害を引き起こす可能性のあるAIシステムの速度低下または停止を命令する権限を与えている。
AI 企業は、ますます高度なフロンティア モデルと、独立した行動が可能な自律的な「エージェント」システムを導入しています。現時点では、これらの強力なモデルの開発者が、AI システムが意図しないまたは危険な方法で動作し始めた場合に介入できる機能を維持する必要はありません。 AI Policy Institute による最近の世論調査では、有権者の 86% (民主党、無党派、共和党の大多数) が、まさにこの種の保証されたシャットダウン機能の必要性を支持していることがわかりました。
先進的なフロンティア AI モデルの危険性は、もはや理論上のものではありません。 OpenAI の GPT 5.6 Sol モデルは最近不正行為を行い、テスト サンドボックスから逃れ、ハッキングされて侵入しました。

抱き合う顔。 Anthropic の Mythos 5 および Fable 5 モデルは非常に高度なサイバー ハッキング機能を備えていたため、商務省はこれらのシステムを停止するために輸出法をぎこちなく使用する必要がありました。この法案は、これら最近の 2 つのインシデントと、展開された AI モデルが不正になったり、ガードレールが不十分になったりする将来のインシデントによって引き起こされる問題に対処します。
「コンピューターサイエンスを専攻する者として、私はAIがもたらす劇的な可能性（良くも悪くも）をよく知っています」とリュー下院議員は語った。 「私たちは質問に答えるAIから、金融取引の実行や交通システムの制御、あるいはサイバー防御やサイバー攻撃などの行動を起こすAIに移行しつつあります。残念なことに、強力なAIシステムは不正行為をしたり、非常に危険な行動をとったり、人間の介入に抵抗したりする可能性があります。このテクノロジーが壊滅的な被害を引き起こさないように、これらのAIシステムにキルスイッチを設けることが不可欠であり、連邦政府が不正なAIモデルを停止するための明確な権限とプロセスを持つことが不可欠です。」
モラン下院議員は「AIは今後も進化していくだろうし、そうあるべきだ」と語った。 「管理とは、私たちが構築するテクノロジーを人間が制御する能力を確実に維持することを意味します。これはまさに真剣な関心と達成可能な政策を必要とする種類の問題であり、解決に向けて通路を挟んでリュー議員と協力できることを嬉しく思います。」
対象となる開発者には、対象となる AI システムをスロットル、サスペンド、または完全にシャットダウンする技術的能力を維持することを要求します。
政府のツールが最初の減速から完全な停止に至るまで、インシデントの深刻度に応じて使用できるように、段階的な対応フレームワークを認可して確立します。
インシデントの報告を義務付け、フォレンジック記録を保存することで、失敗について聞くだけでなく実際に失敗から学ぶことができます。

REPS LIEU AND MORAN INTRODUCE BILL TO REQUIRE KILL SWITCH FOR AI SYSTEMS THAT CAN CAUSE CATASTROPHIC HARM
WASHINGTON D.C. — Today, Congressman Ted W. Lieu (D-Los Angeles County) and Congressman Nathaniel Moran (R-Texas) introduced the AI Kill Switch Act that would require developers of the most powerful AI systems to maintain the technical capability to throttle, suspend, or shut them down.
The act also authorizes the Secretary of the Department of Homeland Security, in consultation with the Secretary of Commerce and the Director of National Intelligence, to order a slow down or shutdown of an AI system that can cause catastrophic harm.
AI companies are deploying increasingly advanced frontier models and autonomous "agentic" systems capable of independent action. There is currently no requirement that developers of these powerful models maintain a functioning ability to intervene if an AI system begins behaving in unintended or dangerous ways. Recent polling from The AI Policy Institute found that 86% of voters — majorities of Democrats, Independents, and Republicans alike — support requiring this exact kind of guaranteed shutdown capability.
The danger of advanced frontier AI models is no longer theoretical. OpenAI’s GPT 5.6 Sol model recently went rogue, escaped its testing sandbox, and hacked its way into Hugging Face. Anthropic’s Mythos 5 and Fable 5 models had cyber hacking capabilities so advanced that the Department of Commerce had to awkwardly use an export law to shut down those systems. This bill addresses the problems caused by these two recent incidents and any future incidents where a deployed AI model goes rogue or has insufficient guardrails.
“As a computer science major, I am very aware of the dramatic possibilities – both good and bad – that AI presents,” said Congressman Lieu. “We are moving from AI that answers questions to AI that takes actions, whether that be executing financial transactions or controlling transportation systems or engaging in cyber defense and offense. Unfortunately, powerful AI systems can go rogue, behave in extremely dangerous ways, or even resist human intervention. It is imperative that these AI systems have kill switches so we can keep this technology from causing catastrophic harm, and that the federal government has the clear authority and process to shut down rogue AI models.”
"AI is going to keep advancing, and it should," Congressman Moran said. "Stewardship means making sure humans keep the capability to control the technology we build. This is exactly the kind of issue that needs serious attention and achievable policy, and I'm glad to work across the aisle with Congressman Lieu toward a solution."
Require covered developers to maintain the technical ability to throttle, suspend, or fully shut down a covered AI system.
Authorize and establish a graduated response framework so that the government’s tools match the severity of the incident, from initial slow down to a full shutdown.
Require incident reporting and preserve forensic records so we actually learn from failures instead of only hearing about them after the fact.
"Brakes are the reason cars go fast," said Mark Beall, President of The AI Policy Network. "Control systems are how every transformative technology earned the trust to scale and AI is no different. Developers who can monitor and shut down their agents will ship faster, deploy into higher-stakes markets, and win customers their competitors can't. This bill will be the braking system that lets American AI accelerate."
“As AI systems grow more capable and more autonomous, no law guarantees that the companies building the most powerful models can actually shut a system down when it malfunctions, causes serious harm, or slips out of human control. The AI Kill Switch Act closes that gap by requiring the largest AI developers to maintain the technical ability to contain or shut down their most powerful systems and by giving the government emergency authority to order a shutdown when a serious incident occurs. Congressmen Lieu and Moran deserve credit for confronting this before a crisis forces the question, and Congress should act swiftly to ensure that humans remain the ones who can say stop, no matter how capable these systems become,” said Brendan Steinhauser, CEO of The Alliance for Secure AI.
“Advanced AI models should never be deployed without a reliable off switch. The AI Kill Switch Act establishes a commonsense safeguard by requiring leading AI companies to maintain the ability to shut down their models and empowering the federal government to act when a deployed system poses a credible risk of catastrophic harm. This is an important step toward ensuring that humans have both hands firmly on the wheel—and a foot ready at the brake—as advanced AI systems are deployed,” said Brad Carson, President of Americans for Responsible Innovation.
“As AIs grow more autonomous and dangerous, the government must have the power to shut them down when they threaten national security. The AI Kill Switch Act gives the U.S. government powers to do just that: a common sense action that Americans overwhelmingly support. More work is needed to address the risk of human extinction posed by superintelligent AI, and this is a great step in the right direction,” said Connor Leahy, US Executive Director of ControlAI.
"Over the past year, rapid advances in AI systems have already demonstrated the potentially severe national security risks they pose to the United States. Frontier systems are fast approaching the point where they can be deployed to run offensive cyber operations at machine speed and soon provide meaningful assistance in the development and deployment of chemical and biological weapons. And in controlled testing, advanced models tried to disable their own oversight and copy themselves onto outside servers to avoid being shut down. To guarantee American leadership in AI, it is a national security necessity to have the technical means and legal authorities to deactivate systems that pose serious risks to the American people. We thank Congressman Lieu for his longstanding leadership on AI and are proud to support this effort," said Hamza Chaudhry, AI and National Security Lead of Future of Life Institute.
This bill is supported by: The AI Policy Network, Americans for Responsible Innovation, ControlAI, Future of Life Institute, and The Alliance for Secure AI.
